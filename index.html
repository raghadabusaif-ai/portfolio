SPARK.UP — Student Center

A dedicated facility near a campus focuses on creating a supportive environment that enhances student life and learning.

What is SPARK.UP?

The project aims to create a vibrant student hub that functions as a "third place," bridging home and university while supporting students' academic, social, and psychological needs. It addresses the lack of supportive campus spaces by integrating formal and informal study areas with dynamic social and activity zones.

The design balances "mind" and "body" through a thoughtful spatial organization, connecting contrasting spaces via courtyards and open areas. Adopting a modern architectural language rooted in local identity, the project emphasizes sustainability through natural lighting and greenery, fostering well-being, a sense of belonging, and community engagement.

Concept

The Student Center is inspired by student behavior, balancing "mind" and "body" through calm, organized study spaces and dynamic, informal areas for movement, interaction, and relaxation.

This duality shapes the design through clear zoning, fluid circulation, and gradual transitions, creating a "third place" between home and university. With green spaces, flexible environments, and intuitive layouts, the center supports both focused study and spontaneous social interaction, adapting to students' evolving needs.

VILLA DESIGN - CONCEPT STATEMENT
Venturi-Inspired Architectural Language
The villa is designed following a Venturi-inspired architectural approach, embracing complexity, contradiction, and expressive form. The concept is reflected through the use of an oversized façade, which acts as a dominant visual element and a defining feature of the project.

Add main project image
Formal Composition & Architectural Elements

The design incorporates a mix of architectural elements, including varied window styles, multiple entrances, and a clearly articulated main entrance, creating a dynamic and layered composition. Wood elements are introduced to add warmth, while cement-based finishes with slightly playful, cartoon-like tones emphasize the expressive character of the design.

ResidentialVilla
Contextual Integration in Amman

Despite the strong influence of Venturi’s architectural language, the project carefully maintains the local identity of Amman. The scale, materials, and overall composition are adapted to harmonize with the surrounding context, ensuring that the villa remains both distinctive and contextually integrated.
